Accessible Selection

What can you do to meet the accessible selection requirement?

When inviting applicants to participate in the selection process, tell candidates that reasonable accommodations for the assessment process are available upon request, whether for an interview, written test, or on-the-job skills demonstration.

When applicants make a request, consult with them about what types of accommodations would work best.

Tips

  • Use standardized interview scripts, questions and scoring to avoid bias among candidates in the interview process.
  • Never assume what a candidate can or cannot do. For example, if a position requires a valid driver’s license, do not assume that a candidate who uses a wheelchair is unable to drive.
